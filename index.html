Program Philosophy

You've got 6 months of D1 strength still in the tank. The goal isn't to train like you're trying to play again — it's to leverage that base while stripping fat. Heavy compounds stay in at moderate volume to preserve muscle. Conditioning ramps up progressively. We're targeting ~2 lbs/week loss, landing you around 240 in 12 weeks while keeping you looking and feeling athletic — not depleted.

Active Recovery
Get outside. Fish the flats, walk the waterfront, paddle, stretch — anything that gets blood moving without taxing the CNS. 30-45 min easy movement. This is where the body actually rebuilds.
🛌
Full Rest
Sleep 8+ hours. Eat well. Hydrate. Meal prep for the week. Your muscles grow here, not in the gym. Don't feel guilty about doing nothing.

Programming Notes

→ Loading: Start main lifts at ~70% of your estimated max. Add 5 lbs/week on upper lifts, 10 lbs/week on lower lifts for the first 6 weeks. After that, hold weight and focus on rep quality.
→ Conditioning ramp: Weeks 1-4 do the listed finishers. Weeks 5-8 add 5 min to each finisher. Weeks 9-12 add a second conditioning session (fasted morning walk, 30 min).
→ Nutrition target: At 264 lbs, aim for ~2,600-2,800 cal/day. Protein at 1g/lb lean mass (~210-220g). Don't crash diet — you'll burn muscle. The deficit comes from training volume.
→ Recovery priority: Sleep > nutrition > training. At 264 with D1 training history, your body can handle volume. But on a caloric deficit, sleep debt will tank your performance and recovery fast.
